Overview

The BV-BRC outbreak pages are designed around genomic data at every step. As new sequences are deposited, automated pipelines annotate coding regions, predict protein changes, and flag signature mutations associated with virulence or resistance. Integrated phylogenomic workflows then place these genomes into regularly updated trees, highlighting emerging clades and pinpointing transmission clusters.

BV-BRC users can drill into metadata-linked genome records, do alignments to examine mutation over time or across geographies, using analysis tools to define outbreak boundaries with genomic precision. Customizable filters let you compare genomes by lineage, host species, or collection date, and export curated sets for downstream analyses.

BV-BRC empowers genomic epidemiologists to track pathogen evolution, infer transmission pathways, and identify novel variants as they arise, all within a reproducible, shareable framework.

Priority Pathogen List

As part of our commitment to providing a one-stop resource for bacterial and viral-pathogen genomics, the BV-BRC team has systematically curated a comprehensive catalog of all recognized viral pathogens. Seen on the Viral Priority Pathogen List tab.

From this master list, we’ve identified a subset of high-priority pathogens, those that pose the greatest risk to public health, either through pandemic potential, high case-fatality rates, or both. These pathogens receive enhanced surveillance, accelerated data integration, and implementable specialized analysis workflows within BV-BRC when an outbreak is coming. This list can be found on the second tab of this page.

Why “High Priority”?

  • Epidemic/Pandemic Potential: These viruses have histories, or strong potential, for rapid spread and large-scale impact.
  • Severe Clinical Outcomes: High case-fatality rates or significant long-term sequelae.
  • Zoonotic Threat: Animal reservoirs that facilitate spillover into human populations.

BV-BRC Response to Outbreaks

For each high-priority pathogen, BV-BRC is working to provide:

  • Dedicated Outbreak Pages with real-time sequence collection and phylogenetics.
  • Variant Annotation Pipelines to pinpoint mutations of concern.
  • Sequence feature variant analysis.
  • Weekly phylogenetic trees
  • Weekly Situation Reports as needed, summarizing newly deposited genomes and phylogenetic analysis.
  • Targeted Webinars & Workshops featuring hands-on tutorials and other ways to add data.

By distinguishing these key viral threats within our broader catalog, BV-BRC enables researchers and public health officials to focus surveillance efforts, streamline analyses, and accelerate actionable insights when they’re needed most.

Outbreaks

Measles 2025 Active

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease that spreads quickly in areas with low vaccination coverage, affecting people of all ages—visit the 2025 measles outbreak page to stay up to date.

Mpox 2024 Active

MPOX is a zoonotic disease endemic to Africa with two main clades—Clade I and Clade II, growing Clade I outbreaks means it is critical to stay informed through our pages.

Influenza H5N1 2024 Active

H5N1 bird flu, driven by the globally spreading 2.3.4.4b strain since 2020, continues to threaten wildlife, agriculture, and human health—making it essential to follow the 2024–2025 H5N1 outbreak page for the latest updates.

SARS-CoV-2

A respiratory virus causing COVID-19—which has spread globally. Actively monitored from 2020–2023—curated global genomes, built lineage dashboards, and monthly variant reports. Monitoring concluded in 2023—review the SARS-CoV-2 outbreak page for a full summary.
